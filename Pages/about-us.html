Mo Tatou / About Us


At The Haukai Restaurant we connect people to the environment, indigeneity, and cultural heritage of Aotearoa through one of life's basic needs: the gathering, cooking and sharing of food; Kai as we call it.


Dining with us is the total Kiwi experience, authentic in its Maori cultural roots, and hand stitched for individuals.


Behind the urban landscape Aotearoa (New Zealand) is rich in kai, in story and in spirit. As the interpreters, we translate the environment and Maori culture to give people the gift of space, rhythm and time to breath out. Our kai nourishes the spirit and the appetite. It is a journey of meaningful travel where the destination is flexible, the experience unique and the memories created are unforgettable.


As our guests you'll have a complete cultural, culinary and Kiwi experience. Certainly, no one ever goes hungry. Storytelling and Maori culture are a big part of the experience. The stories and karakia (song) that you hear will never be forgotten.


Immerse yourself in Maori culture; honoring the memory and wisdom of our ancestors and welcoming the spiritual guidance from our Atua (Gods).



To Tatou Iwi / Our People

The Owners

Jane & Matt Irvine

Jane & Matt have long shared a vision to share the spirit and culture of Aotearoa with the world. With a combined 25 years in hospitality, they are proud to bring you the taste and experience that only New Zealand can offer.

The Chef

Tane Stephens

Having worked all over the world, Tane found himself returning to New Zealand with his young family, eager to revisit some of the recipes from his childhood. The Haukai Restaurant experience is made so incredible by the talent and passion for kai that he brings, along with a strong knowledge of local flora and fauna.

The Guide

Emily Smith

Emily will ensure your visit is as energetic or as tranquil as you wish. Whether it is alone or with a group, you will never forget this unique experience. She is passionate about sharing her culture, and loves to see the excitement in people as they learn more about Maori kai and karakia.
